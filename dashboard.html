JAIIB Syllabus – Latest Pattern

Based on the current IIBF syllabus: 4 compulsory papers and 4 modules (A–D) in each paper.

Paper 1: Indian Economy & Indian Financial System (IE & IFS)

Module A: Indian Economic Architecture

  • Overview of Indian economy – structure (primary, secondary, tertiary sectors)
  • Economic planning in India, NITI Aayog, recent reforms
  • Economic development indicators – poverty, unemployment, inequality
  • Liberalisation, Privatisation, Globalisation (LPG) & economic reforms
  • Government’s role in development, SDGs & inclusive growth

Module B: Economic Concepts Related to Banking

  • Basic micro & macro economics – demand, supply, elasticity
  • Money & inflation – types, causes & control
  • Interest rates – real vs nominal, term structure
  • National income – GDP, GNP, measurement & limitations
  • Business cycles, fiscal policy & monetary policy

Module C: Indian Financial Architecture

  • Structure of Indian financial system – markets, instruments & participants
  • Role of RBI, monetary policy framework & tools
  • Commercial banks, cooperative banks, RRBs, small finance & payments banks
  • NBFCs, DFIs (NABARD, SIDBI, EXIM, etc.) & MFIs
  • Financial market regulators – RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, PFRDA

Module D: Financial Products & Services

  • Money market & capital market instruments (CP, CD, bonds, shares, etc.)
  • Mutual funds – types, NAV, risks & regulation
  • Insurance & pension products – basics of life / general insurance, NPS, APY
  • Derivatives – forwards, futures, options (overview)
  • Factoring, forfaiting, securitisation, REITs & InvITs

Paper 2: Principles & Practices of Banking (PPB)

Module A: General Banking Operations

  • Banker–customer relationship & types of customers
  • Different deposit products – SB, CA, term deposits, NRE/NRO, etc.
  • KYC, AML, CFT & PMLA – risk based approach & reporting
  • Account opening, operation, nomination, closure
  • Customer service, banking ombudsman & grievance redressal

Module B: Functions of Banks

  • Primary & secondary functions of banks – deposits, lending & services
  • Types of advances – fund based & non-fund based
  • Credit appraisal basics – 5 Cs of credit, financial analysis overview
  • Types of securities, modes of charge – lien, pledge, hypothecation, mortgage
  • Priority Sector Lending (PSL), government sponsored schemes
  • NPA concepts, asset classification & recovery channels (SARFAESI, DRT, Lok Adalat)

Module C: Banking Technology

  • Core Banking Systems (CBS) – features & impact on operations
  • Payment systems – NEFT, RTGS, IMPS, UPI, BBPS, cards & wallets
  • Alternate delivery channels – ATM, POS, internet & mobile banking
  • Cyber security risks, information security basics, IT controls
  • Digital products – e-KYC, e-mandates, digital lending basics

Module D: Ethics in Banks & Financial Institutions

  • Concept of business ethics & professional ethics in banking
  • Code of conduct, fair practices code & customer rights
  • Corporate governance in banks & compliance culture
  • Whistleblowing, conflict of interest & handling complaints
  • Ethics and technology – data privacy, confidentiality & security

Paper 3: Accounting & Financial Management for Bankers (AFM)

Module A: Accounting Principles & Processes

  • Basic accounting concepts, principles & standards (including Ind AS – overview)
  • Double entry system, journal, ledger & trial balance
  • Subsidiary books, rectification of errors
  • Depreciation accounting, provisions & reserves
  • Final accounts – trading, P&L and balance sheet basics

Module B: Financial Statements & Core Banking Systems

  • Financial statements of banks – schedules & disclosures
  • Income recognition, provisioning norms (NPA related)
  • Branch vs HO accounting, inter-branch transactions
  • Impact of core banking on accounting & reporting
  • Analysis of financial statements – basics of cash flow & fund flow

Module C: Financial Management

  • Time value of money & basic discounting / compounding
  • Ratio analysis – profitability, liquidity, solvency & efficiency ratios
  • Capital budgeting techniques (NPV, IRR, Payback – concepts)
  • Cost of capital, leverage concepts
  • Working capital management basics

Module D: Taxation & Fundamentals of Costing

  • Basics of direct & indirect taxes relevant to banks
  • TDS / TCS concepts & compliance basics
  • GST overview – place / time / value of supply (basic idea)
  • Costing concepts – cost classification, cost sheet
  • Marginal costing, break-even analysis & budgeting fundamentals

Paper 4: Retail Banking & Wealth Management (RBWM)

Module A: Retail Banking

  • Concept & characteristics of retail banking
  • Retail banking business models & role within bank operations
  • Retail liabilities – SB, CA, term deposits & bundled products
  • Customer profiling & segmentation in retail banking
  • Challenges & future of retail banking

Module B: Retail Products & Recovery

  • Retail asset products – housing, vehicle, personal, education loans, credit cards
  • Documentation, appraisal & risk in retail lending
  • Retail NPAs, recovery & collection process
  • Securitisation & off-balance sheet retail exposures (overview)
  • Consumer protection & grievance redressal in retail products

Module C: Support Services – Marketing of Banking Services / Products

  • Basics of services marketing & 7Ps of marketing for banks
  • Customer relationship management (CRM) & service quality
  • Cross-selling & up-selling of banking / third party products
  • Channels & distribution strategy in retail banking
  • Digital marketing basics for banking products

Module D: Wealth Management

  • Wealth management process & client profiling
  • Asset classes – equity, debt, gold, real estate & alternative products
  • Mutual funds, PMS & other investment products (overview)
  • Retirement & tax planning basics for individuals
  • Suitability, risk profiling & regulatory guidelines
